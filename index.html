Exercise 1 · Daily BriefingResearcher · Critique

How a GM starts the day in 2 minutes. Open Microsoft 365 Copilot at copilot.microsoft.com → click Researcher. Keep the model picker on Auto (this is Critique — dual-model with self-review). Paste the prompt below.

Researcher reads your inbox, calendar, Teams chats, and recent files — then hands General Manager · Retail Operations a single, structured briefing for the day. No more spending the first 30 minutes catching up.

Task 1.1🔍 Researcher · Auto (Critique)

Generate today's GM daily briefing

Create a daily briefing from emails, messages, documents, and meetings to help me prepare for the current workday. Use your understanding of the projects I work on, workstreams I care about, and key people I work with to understand what is relevant and important. Sections: A short intro paragraph that provides an overview of my day, i.e. what a personal assistant would tell me to greet me in the morning. Key updates since yesterday – Include informational updates from the last workday that are likely to impact my work and collaborations. Suggested tasks for today – Tasks or questions assigned to me that I should handle today. Cross-check that suggested tasks haven't been completed already. List unread email threads from the past week that likely need my reply—prioritize those where I'm on To/CC, @mentioned, or involve requests, deadlines, leadership, or customers. Exclude automated messages. Show only urgent or important items in a concise table, and draft replies where needed. If none require action, say: 'I didn't find any unread threads that require a reply.' Meeting prep for today – Upcoming meetings only, shown in a table with: Meeting (time, title, organizer), Topic Overview, and Suggested Preparations. Note if inferred or not needed. Rules: Start with a header: Daily briefing – Day, Date. Follow with a sub-header stating the timestamp of generation. Start each section with a meaningful emoji. Use only explicitly stated facts; no speculation, interpretations or predictions. If a section has no data, state that.
What success looks like: within 90 seconds you have a one-screen brief: overview paragraph, key updates since yesterday, today's tasks, unread threads that need a reply (with drafts), and a meeting-prep table — all grounded on real signals from your tenant, with citations.
Bonus🔍 Researcher · follow-up

Drill in on the single most important item

Pick one item from the briefing that needs deeper thinking. Ask Researcher:

Take the highest-priority item from the briefing above. Pull together everything I've received on it in the last 14 days — emails, chats, files, meeting notes. Summarise the current state in 5 bullets, list the open decisions, and draft one short message I should send today to move it forward.

Exercise 3 · Market Intelligence — Critique & Model CouncilResearcher

When to do this: Right after the daily briefing, when the GM needs deep market intelligence. Open Microsoft 365 Copilot → Researcher.

3.1 · Critique (Auto) — the default for sector intelligence

Researcher on Auto runs Critique mode — a dual-model setup where one model drafts, a second self-reviews the sources, and only the verified report is returned.

Task 3.1🔍 Researcher · Auto (Critique)

Scan the Lifestyle & Sports Retail landscape in Indonesia

Research the Lifestyle & Sports Retail sector in Indonesia. Benchmark these companies: Mitra Adi Perkasa, Erajaya Group, Ace Hardware Indo, Matahari Dept Store, Ramayana, Sogo Indonesia. For each one, compare market share, FY25 revenue growth, EBITDA margin, strategic focus, digital maturity, and recent material moves (M&A, capex, new contracts, regulation). Include 2024–2028 market sizing with the three main growth drivers. Cite every source. Flag any section where the evidence is thin.
Notice the citations and the "reviewed by" stamp — Critique enforces source grounding before the report is shown.

3.2 · Model Council — when the decision is too important for one model

Switch the Researcher model picker to Model Council. Multiple frontier models reason on the same question in parallel — Researcher then reconciles them and shows where they agree, where they diverge, and which sources each one anchored on.

Task 3.2🧑‍⚖️ Researcher · Model Council

Stress-test the strategic question

Model Council — for General Manager · Retail Operations at Contoso Lifestyle Retail: what are the three highest-impact strategic moves we should make in the next 12 months in the Indonesian lifestyle & sports retail sector, given the peer benchmark above? For each move, give: the rationale, the evidence (cited), the main risk, and a 90-day starter action. Show me where the models agree, where they disagree, and which one I should trust on each move.
Why Model Council matters for GMs: for any decision worth more than ~USD 1M, the cost of one model being wrong dwarfs the cost of asking three. Model Council surfaces the disagreement instead of hiding it.

3.3 · Turn the research into a 1-page GM brief

Task 3.3📝 Word Copilot · 1-page brief

Export and refine in Word

In the Researcher report, click Export → Convert to Word. Open the file from OneDrive. In the Word Copilot pane, paste:

Based on the content of this document, draft a 1-page brief for General Manager · Retail Operations at Contoso Lifestyle Retail. Structure: - Market headline (2 sentences, 1 number) - 3 competitor moves we must track - 3 implications for Contoso Lifestyle Retail in the next 90 days - 1 question to put to the Executive Committee Tone: direct, board-ready, no jargon. Max 350 words. Use [PLACEHOLDER] for anything not confirmed in the research. End with assumptions made and one open question.
